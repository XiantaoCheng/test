Scattered electron distribution

By using GEANT4 simulations, we found monoenergetic electrons scattered by a fiducial wire distribute in a Gaussian distribution, $$f \left( \theta \right)=e^{-\theta^{2}/\sigma^{2}}\tag{1}$$ The \(\sigma\) of the Gaussian distribution is a function of incoming electron energy. $$\sigma \left( E,d \right)=A \left( d \right)/E\tag{2}$$
After running a large amount of simulations with fiducials having different diameters, we found the relation, $$A \left( d \right)=207\ d^{0.5582}\tag{3}$$

which means, electron beams with arbitrary spectrum will be scattered in the following distribution, $$f \left( \theta \right)=\sum_{n}{A\ _{n}\ exp \left( -\theta^{2}/\sigma\ _{n}^{2} \right)}\tag{4}$$
