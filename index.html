Refractive index

The response matrix is, $$\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\left(\vec{j_{1}}\right)+v_{x} \frac{\partial}{\partial z}\left(\vec{j_{1}}\right)+\mathbf{T} \vec{j_{1}}=\vec{F}\tag{1}$$ $$-i \omega \vec{j_{1}}+v_{x} i k \vec{j_{1}}+\mathbf{T} \vec{j_{1}}=\vec{F}\tag{2}$$ $$\vec{F}=q n_{e} \vec{E}\tag{3}$$ $$\left(\left(-i \omega+v_{x} i k\right) \mathbf{I}+\mathbf{T}\right) \vec{j_{1}}=q n_{e} \vec{E}\tag{4}$$ $$\vec{j_{1}}=q n_{e} \mathbf{A}^{-1} \vec{E}\tag{4.1}$$ The wave equation is, $$\frac{\omega^{2}}{c^{2}} \vec{E}-\vec{k}^{2} \vec{E}+\left(\vec{k} \vec{k}\right)\cdot \vec{E}=-i \omega \mu_{0} \vec{j_{1}}\tag{5}$$ $$\frac{\omega^{2}}{c^{2}} \vec{E}-\vec{k}^{2} \vec{E}+\left(\vec{k} \vec{k}\right)\cdot \vec{E}=-i \omega \mu_{0} q n_{e} \mathbf{A}^{-1} \vec{E}\tag{6}$$ If we choose eigenvectors of matrix A, which is perpendicular to \(\vec{k}\), then all matrices in the equation will become a number, $$\frac{\omega^{2}}{c^{2}}-k^{2}=-i \omega \mu_{0} q n_{e}/\left(-i \omega+t_{i}\right)\tag{7}$$ (Ignore v_x*k for simplification) $$\frac{\omega^{2}}{c^{2}}-k^{2}=-\frac{i \omega \mu_{0} q n_{e}}{-i \omega+t_{i}}\tag{8}$$ $$1-n^{2}=\frac{c^{2}}{\omega^{2}} \frac{\omega \mu_{0} q n_{e}}{\omega+i t_{i}}\tag{9}$$ $$n^{2}=1-\frac{c^{2}}{\omega^{2}} \frac{\omega \mu_{0} q n_{e}}{\omega+i t_{i}}\tag{10}$$ $$n=\sqrt{1-\frac{\omega_{p}^{2}}{\omega^{2}} \frac{1}{1+i t_{i}/\omega}}\tag{11}$$ $$n=\sqrt{1-\frac{\omega_{p}^{2}}{\omega^{2}} \frac{\omega^{2}-i t_{i} \omega}{\omega^{2}+t_{i}^{2}}}\tag{12}$$ (There is a problem with this result) Now we re-consider the v_x*k term, $$n^{2}=1-\frac{\omega_{p}^{2}}{\omega^{2}} \frac{\omega}{\omega-v_{x} k+i t_{i}}\tag{13}$$ $$n^{2}=1-\frac{\omega_{p}^{2}}{\omega^{2}} \frac{1}{1-\beta_{x} n+i t_{i}/\omega}\tag{14}$$ $$n^{2} \left(1-\beta_{x} n+i t_{i}/\omega\right)=\left(1-\beta_{x} n+i t_{i}/\omega\right)-\frac{\omega_{p}^{2}}{\omega^{2}}\tag{15}$$ $$-\beta_{x} n^{3}+\left(1+i t_{i}/\omega+\beta_{x}\right) n=\left(1+i t_{i}/\omega\right)-\frac{\omega_{p}^{2}}{\omega^{2}}\tag{16}$$ $$n^{3}-\left(1+i t_{i}/\omega+\beta_{x}\right)/\beta_{x} n+\left(1+i t_{i}/\omega-\frac{\omega_{p}^{2}}{\omega^{2}}\right)/\beta_{x}=0\tag{17}$$ One of the roots of a cubic equation is, $$n^{3}+p n+q=0\tag{18}$$ $$n=\left(-\frac{q}{2}+\sqrt{\left(\frac{q}{2}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{p}{3}\right)^{3}}\right)^{1/3}+\left(-\frac{q}{2}-\sqrt{\left(\frac{q}{2}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{p}{3}\right)^{3}}\right)^{1/3}\tag{19}$$ Here we have, $$p=-\left(1+i t_{i}/\omega+\beta_{x}\right)/\beta_{x}\tag{20}$$ $$q=\left(1+i t_{i}/\omega-\frac{\omega_{p}^{2}}{\omega^{2}}\right)/\beta_{x}\tag{21}$$
