NS equations

$$\frac{\partial \rho}{\partial t}+\vec{\nabla}\cdot \left(\rho \vec{v}\right)=0\tag{1}$$ $$\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\left(\rho \vec{v}\right)+\vec{\nabla}\cdot \left(\rho \vec{v} \vec{v}\right)=\vec{F}\tag{2}$$ In our cases, the temperature is assumed to be zero, so the energy conservation is same as the momentum conservation. And we studied the perturbation current caused by probe fields, $$\vec{v}=\vec{v_{0}}+\vec{v_{1}}\tag{3}$$ $$\vec{j_{1}}=\rho \vec{v_{1}}\tag{4}$$ Then equations become, $$\vec{\nabla}\cdot \vec{j_{1}}=0\tag{5}$$ $$\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\left(\vec{j_{1}}\right)+\vec{\nabla}\cdot \left(\vec{v_{0}} \vec{j_{1}}+\vec{j_{1}} \vec{v_{0}}\right)=\vec{F}\tag{6}$$ $$\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\left(\vec{j_{1}}\right)+\vec{\nabla}\cdot \left(\vec{v_{0}} \vec{j_{1}}\right)+\vec{\nabla}\cdot \left(\vec{j_{1}} \vec{v_{0}}\right)=\vec{F}\tag{7}$$ $$\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\left(\vec{j_{1}}\right)+\vec{\nabla}\cdot \left(\vec{v_{0}}\right) \vec{j_{1}}+\left(\vec{v_{0}}\cdot \vec{\nabla}\right) \vec{j_{1}}+\vec{\nabla}\cdot \left(\vec{j_{1}} \vec{v_{0}}\right)=\vec{F}\tag{8}$$ $$\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\left(\vec{j_{1}}\right)+\left(\vec{\nabla}\cdot \vec{v_{0}}\right) \vec{j_{1}}+\left(\vec{v_{0}}\cdot \vec{\nabla}\right) \vec{j_{1}}+\left(\vec{\nabla}\cdot \vec{j_{1}}\right) \vec{v_{0}}+\left(\vec{j_{1}}\cdot \vec{\nabla}\right) \vec{v_{0}}=\vec{F}\tag{9}$$ $$\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\left(\vec{j_{1}}\right)+\left(\vec{\nabla}\cdot \vec{v_{0}}\right) \vec{j_{1}}+\left(\vec{v_{0}}\cdot \vec{\nabla}\right) \vec{j_{1}}+\left(\vec{j_{1}}\cdot \vec{\nabla}\right) \vec{v_{0}}=\vec{F}\tag{10}$$

Probe field perturbations

Assuming that the probe beam is along the x-axis, then this equation can be farther simplified by considering following condition, $$\vec{\nabla} \vec{j_{1}}\approx \hat{x} \frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left(\vec{j_{1}}\right)\tag{11}$$ Then $$\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\left(\vec{j_{1}}\right)+v_{x} \frac{\partial}{\partial z}\left(\vec{j_{1}}\right)+\left(\vec{\nabla}\cdot \vec{v_{0}}\right) \vec{j_{1}}+\left(\vec{j_{1}}\cdot \vec{\nabla}\right) \vec{v_{0}}=\vec{F}\tag{12}$$ $$\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\left(\vec{j_{1}}\right)+v_{x} \frac{\partial}{\partial z}\left(\vec{j_{1}}\right)+\mathbf{T} \vec{j_{1}}=\vec{F}\tag{13}$$ The matrix is, $$\mathbf{T}=\begin{pmatrix} \frac{\partial v_{x}}{\partial x}&\frac{\partial v_{x}}{\partial y}&\frac{\partial v_{x}}{\partial z}\\ \frac{\partial v_{y}}{\partial x}&\frac{\partial v_{y}}{\partial y}&\frac{\partial v_{y}}{\partial z}\\ \frac{\partial v_{z}}{\partial x}&\frac{\partial v_{z}}{\partial y}&\frac{\partial v_{z}}{\partial z} \end{pmatrix} +\left(\frac{\partial v_{x}}{\partial x}+\frac{\partial v_{y}}{\partial y}+\frac{\partial v_{z}}{\partial z}\right) \mathbf{I}\tag{14}$$ We should also simplify this, $$\mathbf{T}=\begin{pmatrix} \frac{\partial v_{x}}{\partial x}&\frac{\partial v_{x}}{\partial y}&0\\ \frac{\partial v_{y}}{\partial x}&\frac{\partial v_{y}}{\partial y}&0\\ 0&0&0 \end{pmatrix} +\left(\frac{\partial v_{x}}{\partial x}+\frac{\partial v_{y}}{\partial y}\right) \mathbf{I}\tag{15}$$ $$\mathbf{T}=\begin{pmatrix} 2 \frac{\partial v_{x}}{\partial x}+\frac{\partial v_{y}}{\partial y}&\frac{\partial v_{x}}{\partial y}&0\\ \frac{\partial v_{y}}{\partial x}&\frac{\partial v_{x}}{\partial x}+2 \frac{\partial v_{y}}{\partial y}&0\\ 0&0&\frac{\partial v_{x}}{\partial x}+\frac{\partial v_{y}}{\partial y} \end{pmatrix} \tag{16}$$ $$\mathbf{T}=\begin{pmatrix} A&B&0\\ C&D&0\\ 0&0&E \end{pmatrix} \tag{17}$$ The eigenvalues can be calculated by solving the equation, $$\left|\begin{pmatrix} A-t&B&0\\ C&D-t&0\\ 0&0&E-t \end{pmatrix} \right|=0\tag{18}$$ $$\left(D-t\right) \left(E-t\right) \left(A-t\right)-B \left(E-t\right) C=0\tag{19}$$ $$t^{2}-\left(A+D\right) t+A D-B C=0\tag{20}$$ The solution is, $$t=\frac{A+D\pm \sqrt{\left(A+D\right)^{2}-4 \left(A D-B C\right)}}{2}\tag{21}$$ $$t=\frac{A+D\pm \sqrt{\left(A-D\right)^{2}+4 B C}}{2}\tag{22}$$ And eigenvectors are, $$t a=a A+b B\tag{23}$$ $$a=\frac{B}{t-A} b\tag{24}$$ $$a=\frac{B}{\frac{A+D\pm \sqrt{\left(A-D\right)^{2}+4 B C}}{2}-A} b\tag{25}$$ $$a=\frac{-2 B}{\left(A-D\right)\mp \sqrt{\left(A-D\right)^{2}+4 B C}} b\tag{26}$$ $$a=\frac{-2 B \left(\left(A-D\right)\pm \sqrt{\left(A-D\right)^{2}+4 B C}\right)}{-4 B C} b\tag{27}$$ $$a=\frac{\left(A-D\right)\pm \sqrt{\left(A-D\right)^{2}+4 B C}}{2 C} b\tag{28}$$

Quasi-static approximation

Let's use a new coordinates to describe equations, $$\begin{cases} s=t\\ \eta=z-c t \end{cases} \tag{29}$$ $$\begin{cases} \frac{\partial }{\partial t}=\frac{\partial s}{\partial t} \frac{\partial }{\partial s}+\frac{\partial \eta}{\partial t} \frac{\partial }{\partial \eta}\\ \frac{\partial }{\partial z}=\frac{\partial s}{\partial z} \frac{\partial }{\partial s}+\frac{\partial \eta}{\partial z} \frac{\partial }{\partial \eta} \end{cases} \tag{30}$$ $$\begin{cases} \frac{\partial }{\partial t}=\frac{\partial }{\partial s}-c \frac{\partial }{\partial \eta}\\ \frac{\partial }{\partial z}=\frac{\partial }{\partial \eta} \end{cases} \tag{31}$$ Then eqn.[13] will becomes, $$\frac{\partial}{\partial s}\left(\vec{j_{1}}\right)+\left(v_{x}-c\right) \frac{\partial}{\partial \eta}\left(\vec{j_{1}}\right)+\mathbf{T} \vec{j_{1}}=\vec{F}\tag{32}$$ By using the approximation, \(\partial j/\partial s\approx 0\), we have, $$\left(v_{x}-c\right) \frac{\partial}{\partial \eta}\left(\vec{j_{1}}\right)+\mathbf{T} \vec{j_{1}}=\vec{F}\tag{33}$$ OK, let's put \(F=F_0 exp(i k \eta)\) into the equation, $$\left(v_{x}-c\right) \frac{\partial j_{1}}{\partial \eta}+t_{1} j_{1}=F_{0} exp \left( i k \eta \right)\tag{34}$$ $$j_{1}=A exp \left( i k \eta \right)\tag{35}$$ $$\left(v_{x}-c\right) i k A+t_{1} A=F_{0}\tag{36}$$ So, $$A=\frac{F_{0}}{t_{1}+i \left(v_{x}-c\right) k}\tag{37}$$
